Skiing Around the World

The first place is St Moritz in Switzerland. A luxury ski resort with 58 lifts and 350km of runs, it is a must visit if you love skiing and expensive resorts, as it is the second most expensive place in the world for tourism. St Moritz has hosted the winter olympics twice, and has an outdoor olympic ice rink. With lots of cross country and off piste terrain, it has something for everyone, although you have to be extremely rich to visit.

Val d'Isère is a ski resort in the French Alps near the Italian border. It is part of the Espace Killy ski area, and home to many difficult runs such as the steep Face de Bellevarde and the Grande Motte glacier, which is great for summer skiing. Val d'Isère also has lots of bike tracks which are popular in the summer.

St Anton am Arlberg is an Austrian village in the Tyrolean Alps. It's known as a gateway to the Arlberg ski region and is often called the “cradle of alpine skiing” for its role in inventing the sport. The Museum St. Anton am Arlberg shows the local history of skiing in a traditional chalet. 88 lifts and cable cars provide access to the slopes of Valluga and Rendl. The village is also known for its lively après-ski scene.
